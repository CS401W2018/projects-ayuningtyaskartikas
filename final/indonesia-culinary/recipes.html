1. Nasi Goreng Recipe

Nasi Goreng is a very-well known Indonesian dish. It is one of the 30 icons of Indonesian cuisine. "Nasi" means rice and "Goreng" means fried, it is a simple and delicious dish that can be enjoy in every occasion.

Ingredients

  • cooked cold rice - 400gr

  • kecap manis (sweet soy) - 2tsp

  • shallots - 2 cloves

  • oil - 1tsp

  • garlic - 3 cloves

  • chili - 3pcs

  • chicken - 200gr

  • 2 eggs

  • salt

  • green onion

  • oyster sauce

How to cook

1. Crush the garlic then saute until fragrant, add the eggs, scramble until cooked.

2. Add the rice, chicken, sweet soy sauce, oyster sauce, and salt then stir well. Add spring onions and sliced ​​chilies, stir well.

3. Cook until the spices are absorbed, then remove and serve with fried onions.

2. Rendang Recipe

Rendang is a typical Indonesian food that has gone global, it has become one of the culinary icons of Indonesia. Because this dish is rich in so many spices and takes a long time to cook, making this food rich in flavor and recognized as one of the most delicious foods in the world.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef

  • 3 coconuts

  • 4 lime leaves

  • 1 turmeric leaf, tied

  • 3 stalks of lemongrass

  • 4 pieces of kandis acid

  • 2 tsp salt

  • 100 g ambu/roasted coconut

  • 1 tbsp rendang spices

  • 250 g large red chilies

  • 20 g galangal

  • 100 g shallots

  • 100 g garlic

  • 50 g candlenuts

  • 3 cm turmeric

  • 2 cm ginger

How to cook

1. Cut the beef into 3 cm cubes. Set aside

2. Saute the ground spices with 4 tablespoons of cooking oil until fragrant.

3. Add lime leaves, lemongrass, turmeric leaves, kandis acid, and salt, then stir well.

4. Pour in the coconut milk, add the meat spices and cook until boiling.

5. Add the pieces of meat, cook over low heat for a few hours while stirring occasionally until the coconut milk thickens, becomes oily and is slightly dry brown.

3. Martabak Manis Recipe

Martabak is also commonly known as Indonesian pancake. Sweet and thick Indonesian version of pancake with various fillings. The most sought after are pancakes with chocolate and peanut fillings. The texture is soft and chewy with a sweet and savory taste.

Ingredients

  • 250 grams of medium protein wheat flour

  • 4 tablespoons granulated sugar

  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda

  • 1 egg

  • 300 ml full cream liquid milk

  • Butter and Sugar

  • Chocolate sprinkles

  • Cheese

  • Sweetened condensed milk

How to cook

1. Prepare a container. Add flour, granulated sugar, salt, eggs, and full cream liquid milk. Stir until evenly mixed or smooth.

2. After that, cover the container with a cloth or plastic wrap and let stand for 1 hour.

3. Then add yeast to the dough. Stir again for about 2 minutes to mix.

4. Meanwhile, you can heat a Teflon on the stove. When it is hot, add the martabak dough and use medium heat when cooking.

5. Wait until bubbles appear, add enough granulated sugar or according to taste.

6. Reduce the heat and cover the Teflon while occasionally opening it to see the doneness of the martabak or the dough changes color and the skin is cooked. Remove and set aside.

7. Spread butter on top of the martabak and add cheese, chocolate sprinkles, and sweetened condensed milk to taste. Or you can replace other toppings according to your and your family members' preferences at home, Moms.

4. Es Doger Recipe

Es Doger is a traditional Indonesian drink originating from Cirebon. With a sweet and fresh taste, Es Doger is really good to be drink during summer time.

Es Doger is known for its mixture of shaved ice, coconut milk, and various appetizing fillings.

Ingredients

  • Coconut milk (amount to taste)

  • Granulated sugar (to taste)

  • Cocopandan syrup (optional)

  • Salt to taste

  • Cassava tapai

  • Grated young coconut

  • Shaved ice

How to cook

1. Boil Coconut Milk: In a saucepan, boil coconut milk, granulated sugar, and salt until boiling. Stir continuously so that the coconut milk does not split.

2. Add Flavor: If you like, add syrup and cocopandan paste to give an attractive aroma and color. Stir well.

3. Cool: After all ingredients are well mixed, remove from heat and let cool.

4. Prepare a glass, add shaved ice until full. Pour the chilled coconut milk mixture over the shaved ice. Add cassava tapai and grated young coconut as a complement.
