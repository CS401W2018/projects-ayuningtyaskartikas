Background

Indonesian food has evolved over centuries through the influences of trade, migration, and colonialism. Indigenous peoples in Indonesia initially developed their own culinary traditions, but with the arrival of foreign traders and colonizers, these influences shaped the diverse flavors we see today.

As a result, Indonesian food today is a blend of these cultural influences, creating a unique and rich culinary heritage.

Indonesia is an archipelago country, consists more than 17,000 islands and more than 600 ethnic groups.

If you visit Indonesia you will realize how diverse is this country, not only the etnicities, language but also the foods.
